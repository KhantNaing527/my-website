Electronic Conﬁguration
Deﬁnition:
Electronic conﬁguration describes the arrangement of electrons in an atom's orbitals. It helps to
understand the chemical behavior of elements.
Principles of Electronic Conﬁguration
1. Aufbau Principle:
Electrons ﬁll orbitals starting from the lowest energy level to the highest.
The order of ﬁlling is determined by increasing energy levels: , , , , , , , ,
, and so on.
2. Pauli Exclusion Principle:
No two electrons in an atom can have the same set of four quantum numbers.
An orbital can hold a maximum of two electrons with opposite spins.
3. Hund's Rule:
Electrons will ﬁll degenerate orbitals (orbitals with the same energy) singly ﬁrst before
pairing up.
This minimizes electron-electron repulsion within an atom.

Relevant Concepts
1. Periodic Table:
Electronic conﬁgurations explain the structure of the periodic table.
Elements in the same group have similar valence electron conﬁgurations, leading to similar
chemical properties.
2. Valence Electrons:
Electrons in the outermost shell.
Determine an element's chemical properties and reactivity.
3. Orbital Shapes and Energies:
s-orbitals: Spherical
p-orbitals: Dumbbell-shaped
d- and f-orbitals: More complex shapes.
Common Mistakes and Corrections
1. Incorrect Order of Filling:
Mistake: Not following the correct order of orbital ﬁlling.
Correction: Use the Aufbau principle and refer to an energy level diagram.
2. Ignoring Hund's Rule:
Mistake: Pairing electrons in degenerate orbitals before all orbitals are singly occupied.
Correction: Ensure that electrons occupy all degenerate orbitals singly before pairing.
3. Miscounting Electrons:
Mistake: Incorrectly counting the number of electrons, leading to incorrect conﬁgurations.
Correction: Carefully count the total number of electrons and distribute them according to
the principles.
4. Incorrect Use of Noble Gas Notation:
Mistake: Using the wrong noble gas or not including all remaining electrons.
Correction: Ensure the correct noble gas is used and add all subsequent electrons
accurately.
