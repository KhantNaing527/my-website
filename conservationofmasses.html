Conservation of Masses
Deﬁnition:
The law of conservation of mass states that mass in an isolated system is neither created nor
destroyed by chemical reactions or physical transformations. The mass of the products in a chemical
reaction must equal the mass of the reactants.
Key Concepts
1. Isolated System:
A system where neither matter nor energy can enter or leave. The total mass remains
constant.
2. Closed System:
A system where energy can enter or leave, but matter cannot. The mass remains constant
during chemical reactions.
3. Balanced Chemical Equations:
Representation of a chemical reaction with equal numbers of each type of atom on both
sides of the equation, ensuring mass conservation.
Principles of Conservation of Mass
1. Chemical Reactions:
In any chemical reaction, the total mass of reactants equals the total mass of products.

2. Physical Changes:
Mass is conserved during physical changes like melting, boiling, or dissolving.
Example: Melting ice to water; the mass of ice equals the mass of water produced.
Demonstrating Conservation of Mass
1. Chemical Reactions:
Conduct a reaction in a closed container and measure the mass before and after the
reaction.
Example: Reacting vinegar (acetic acid) with baking soda (sodium bicarbonate) in a sealed
bag. Measure the mass before and after the reaction; it should remain the same.
2. Physical Changes:
Measure the mass of a substance before and after a physical change.
Example: Dissolving salt in water and measuring the total mass before and after
dissolution.

1. Stoichiometry:
The calculation of reactants and products in chemical reactions based on the conservation
of mass.
Example: Calculating how much product will form from given amounts of reactants.

Common Mistakes and Corrections
1. Unbalanced Equations:
Mistake: Not having the same number of each type of atom on both sides of the equation.
Correction: Use coeﬃcients to balance the equation.
2. Ignoring Mass of Gases:
Mistake: Not accounting for the mass of gases in reactions.
Correction: Ensure the mass of gases is included in the total mass.
3. Incorrect Counting of Atoms:
Mistake: Miscounting the number of atoms of each element.
Correction: Double-check the count of each type of atom in reactants and products.
4. Neglecting Conservation in Physical Changes:
Mistake: Assuming mass is not conserved in physical changes.
Correction: Remember that mass is conserved in both physical and chemical changes.
Summary
Conservation of Mass: Mass is neither created nor destroyed in a closed system.
Key Concepts: Isolated system, closed system, balanced equations, stoichiometry, limiting and
excess reactants.
Demonstration: Conduct reactions and measure mass before and after.
Common Mistakes: Unbalanced equations, ignoring gases, incorrect atom counting, neglecting
physical changes.
